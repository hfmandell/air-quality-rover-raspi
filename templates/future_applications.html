Future Applications

Leveraging technology for environmental science.

Data Automation
As it is currently deployed, the data collection, storage, and visualization of the rover is entirely manual. The user must start the RaspberyPi's program, move the rover around, kill the program, send the GPS and airquality data to their main computer, and only then can the data be processed and deployed to the website.
Luckily, we live in an increasingly automated society. With the tools of Cloud computing and IoT this process could be completely automated as long as a wifi or bluetooth connection exists. Since the RPi is considered to be a 'less capable device', it would be connected via 'gateway' to a cloud 'Internet of Technology' (IoT) setup.
Once the gateway is in place, the RPi can be registered on Cloud IoT and configurations can set up a Pub/Sub system to ingest, process, and store data accordingly.
Rover Automation
The locomotion of the rover is also currently manual. The user must be close enough to the rover such that the remote can communicate with the rover's antenna. Thus, the rover's path and exploration is limited by the mobility of the user. This is highly impractical to use over a long period of time.
The rover itself can be transformed into an automated vehicle. By adding a camera, the rover gains vision with the help of AI-powered software. Connecting the vision to the rover's motor builds a fully automated device that can traverse diverse terrain, driving itself along a unique path or following a pre-programmed path safely. This sort of hands-off monitoring is safest and most effective for long term, regular data collection.
At Pomona College, this will be pursued in the ARCS (Autonomous Robotics and Complex Systems) Lab, where robust robots are studied and created under the guidance of Professor Anthony Clark.
Community Engagement
Community science efforts have become fairly common. Groups work together to clean up street litter, monitor local water sources, or even advocate for local recycling efforts. Air quality is an important environmental health metric because it immediately impacts both human beings and the natural world.
An air quality monitoring robot could be utilized by community groups (local governments, elementary schools, volunteer organizations) to contribute to larger air quality databases or to simply serve as a lesson on everyday particulate pollution.
